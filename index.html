ABOUT




Technically, my name is Ricardo, but call me Ricky. I am an undergraduate student at UC Berkeley studying Computer Science and Film. I have a deep passion for computer graphics; from the theory to its artistic applications, I am enthusiastic for anything CG, but particularly its role in Animation and other film media.

I have been fortunate enough to work on a few short 3D animated films while being a facilitator for UCBUGG: 3D Modeling & Animation. At the moment, I am a research assistant on the NOME project, supervised by Professor Carlo Sequin. My favorite graphics topics are light transport and rendering.

SHORT FILMS




Brilliance (2018)

The UCBUGG staff got together and created this wonderful short film about an italian man with electricity problems. I did most of the lighting for this short, as well as sets and camera. This was one of my favorite projects to light up!

Lighting night scenes was a challenge that took many iterations, so I am very pleased with the end result.

Only Love (2017)

This project was made for UC Berkeley's Advanced Digital Animation course, CNM190 (Fall 2016 - Spring 2017). In essence, its a short about the pangs of alcoholism, but very layered, revealing new meanings with every view.

I worked on the sound effects, lighting, modeling, and post-production for this short. Specifically, the lighting I am most proud of is during the liquor store scene, as we experiemented with what felt like every possible variation. Since we were going for a Tell-Tale -chiaroscuro- comic book-esque look, directional lights were crucial.

I am really proud of the music I wrote for this, especially when I found my peers humming the music at random times. Only Love has won an award at the East LA film festival, and is being considered for other film festivals!
